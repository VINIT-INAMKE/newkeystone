Rules And Regulations

PRIMARY FUNCTIONS OF EXAMINATION CELL

  • 1.

    Guide the exam section to conduct the University exams smoothly as per the regulations by the SPPU University.

  • 2.

    Examination Cell serves all Examination notices received from University to all concerned departments within the stipulated time.

  • 3.

    Examination cell prepares Circulars for students regarding Exam form uploading.

  • 4.

    Scrutiny of Examination Application Forms and issue of Hall Tickets.

  • 5.

    Circulation of SPPU examination Timetable to respective departments.

  • 6.

    Displaying the Results

  • 7.

    Report Students grievances to SPPU

  • 8.

    To guide the students appearing for SPPU University exams.

  • 9.

    Monitoring the exam stationeries received from the university and records of its uses and submits the report of the same to University.

  • 10.

    Report the malpractices during the examination if any to the SPPU University.

  • 11.

    To guide and monitor the conduction of practical and theory exams as per the schedule of SPPU University exams.

  • 12.

    To execute the orders/circulars received from the controller of exams (SPPU) on time and take necessary actions.

ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES OF EXAM CELL

  • 1.

    To provide paper format to departments to distribute to each faculty with a deadline for submission.

  • 2.

    Paper typing and get them verified by faculties for printing.

  • 3.

    Preparation of Time table, Invigilation duty chart, seating arrangement in the examination halls etc. and display/convey to appropriate place/member.

  • 4.

    To appoint internal Flying Squad, Supervisors and other human resources for smooth conduction of examination.

  • 5.

    Ensuring on-time reporting of faculty in exam cell, opening and closing of examination-rooms and availability of adequate stationery, like answer sheets, drawing sheets, charts, graph paper, drawing boards.

  • 6.

    Collection of reports from each examination-room and prepare a consolidated report regarding students

  • 7.

    Appear/Absent for each paper of each class and submit it to HODs and Director.

  • 8.

    After completion of examination, distribution of answer books to the concerned teachers and receiving the answer books and award list, and preparing in the desired format within a predefined deadline

  • 9.

    Prepare cumulative result for each branch/section and provide it to HODs for analysis for follow-up action.

  • 10.

    After declaration of results, the Exam Cell shall display notice inviting students who desire a re-totalling /revaluation of their answer sheets.

  • 11.

    To make examinations forms available to students and collect filled forms with fees and send them to university (Accounts-section).

  • 12.

    Prepare seating plan and put a sticker of roll no. at specific bench and display the seating arrangement outside room and also at entry gate.

  • 13.

    Make duty plan and ensure faculty reporting at least 30 minutes before exam and provide them examination sheets with seating plan and display the regulations for invigilators.

  • 14.

    Make a folder of papers for each room and distribute them to invigilators before 5 minutes of start of exam.

  • 15.

    Collection of students entry sheet after 30 minutes of start of exam.

  • 16.

    Collection of copies (after counting according to statement) and collecting the statements of each room.

  • 17.

    Sealing of copies bunch and pasting a statement on packed bundle.

  • 18.

    Make data entry regarding absentees and present candidates and send it to university.

  • 19.

    Download the result of each student and take print to make a file semester and year-wise.

  • 20.

    After getting the original mark-sheet from university, distribute them to students.

INSTRUCTIONS TO SUPERVISORS

  • 1.

    All the Invigilators should report to the Centre Superintendent half an hour before the commencement of each Examination.

  • 2.

    All the Invigilators should reach the Examination Hall at least 15 minutes before the commencement of each Examination.

  • 3.

    The Invigilator should be VIGILANT throughout the duration of the Examination.

  • 4.

    The Invigilators should ensure that no candidate shall carry any type of unauthorized material in the Examination Hall.

  • 5.

    No Candidates should be permitted in the Examination Hall without Hall Ticket.

  • 6.

    The Answer Script should be handed over to the Candidate who possesses proper Hall Ticket only.

  • 7.

    The Question Paper should be given to the candidate as per the timetable and the date/time mentioned on the envelope of the Question Papers.

  • 8.

    The Invigilators are requested to maintain high standard of discipline in the Examination Hall. No Candidate shall be permitted to take the Examination by any unfair means. No Candidate shall be permitted to talk in the Examination Hall.

  • 9.

    It is the primary duty of the Invigilator to ensure the fair conduct of Examinations. Non compliance shall attract disciplinary action.

  • 10.

    No Candidate should be permitted in the Examination Hall after 15 minutes of the commencement of the Examination.

  • 11.

    No Candidate should be permitted to leave the Examination Hall till the completion of the Examination time.

  • 12.

    The Invigilator should ensure that the Enrolment Number entered by the Candidate on the answer booklet must be correct and in proper format.

  • 13.

    The Invigilator should take the attendance of the Candidates on the printed Room Report after 15 minutes of the commencement of the Examination. Observer/Flying Squad visiting the Examination Hall shall verify the Room Report.

  • 14.

    All absent cases should be distinctly written in red ink in the Room Report.

  • 15.

    The invigilator should cautiously verify the identity of the Candidate with his Hall Ticket and compare his/her particulars with the ID Card of the Candidate and the duplicate Hall Ticket provided by the Centre Superintendent.

  • 16.

    The Invigilator should verify all the entries on the Answer Script of the Candidate and sign the Answer Script. The Candidate should be asked to fill all the blanks on the answer sheet. (Answer scripts without the signature and name of the Invigilators shall NOT be evaluated.)

  • 17.

    There shall be no additional Answer Scripts for those writing in new format of answer booklets containing 32 pages.

  • 18.

    If any Candidate caught with any unauthorized material, he should be immediately debarred from that Examination. The unauthorized material should be tagged properly to the Answer Script, and the front page of the script should be marked across in red as ‘Malpractice Case’.

  • 19.

    All Malpractice cases should be reported to the Centre Superintendent separately.

  • 20.

    Candidate caught in malpractice cases should be immediately moved out of the Examination premises.

  • 21.

    The Invigilator should announce the time at each hour and at the last half an hour.

  • 22.

    No Candidate should be permitted to write anything on the Answer Script after the completion of the Examination time.

  • 23.

    Immediately after the completion of the Examination, the Invigilators should handover the Answer Scripts, Room Report, remaining question papers, stationary items, details of MP cases, etc., to the Centre Superintendent and obtain acknowledgement.

  • 24.

    The Observer at the Examination Centre and Flying Squad on surprise visits shall be asked to submit their report on the duties performed by the Invigilators.

INSTRUCTIONS TO CANDIDATES/STUDENTS

  • 1.

    Candidates are required to be present at the examination centre, FIFTEEN MINUTES before the time fixed for the first paper and TEN MINUTES before the time of each subsequent paper.

  • 2.

    Candidates are forbidden from taking any material into the examination hall, that can be used for malpractice at the time of examination.

  • 3.

    Candidates are requested to see the Notice Board at their place of examination regularly for changes if any, that may be notified later in the program.

  • 4.

    No request for any special concession such as a change in time or any day fixed for the University Examination on any ground shall be granted.

  • 5.

    Candidates are requested to note the Day, Date and Time of every Paper on every day.

  • 6.

    Candidates are permitted to use stencils at the time of examination.

  • 7.

    Candidates appearing for the examinations are expected to provide themselves with Side- rules.

  • 8.

    The exchange or loan of side-rules, drawing instruments of other materials used in the examination hall is Not Permitted while the examinations are in process. Candidates must bring their own instruments and will not be allowed to borrow from each other under any circumstances.

  • 9.

    Use of non-programmable battery, operated electronic calculator of Pocket-size is allowed. The exchange of Calculators is not allowed.
